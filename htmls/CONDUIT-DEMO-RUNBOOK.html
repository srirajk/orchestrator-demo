The plan — what you're proving in ~8 minutes

Before the clicks: the story you're telling, so you steer it instead of just reading it.

The thesis (say it however feels natural): "This isn't a chatbot bolted onto a bank. It's a gateway that understands the conversation, routes each question to the right specialist system, enforces exactly what each person is allowed to see, and shows its work — and it does all of that without one line of code that knows anything about wealth or insurance."

Everything you do lands one of three proofs, in order:

1 · It follows the conversation like a person. Act 1 Follow-ups — "their performance?", "now Calderon", "compare them" — just work, with no re-typing the client each time. This is the part that makes people lean in.

2 · The same engine runs any business line — with zero domain code. Act 2 Log in as an insurance underwriter and it behaves identically. Onboarding insurance was a manifest + a data service, not a release. That's the moat: anyone can build a chatbot; this is a manifest interpreter.

3 · You can trust it, and it stays in control. Acts 1 & 3 · closer It never invents a number or an account (grounded answers only); each person sees only their own book ("Okafor → not in your coverage"); and if a backend agent dies mid-answer, it degrades honestly instead of failing. Every decision is visible in the glass-box, and every answer is costed.

Why two characters: rm_jane (wealth) shows the depth — the conversational reasoning. uw_sam (insurance) shows the breadth — same gateway, new business line, no code. The switch between them is the point: watch how little changes on our side.

If a step doesn't land — fallback moves

Terse one-word turns can vary. A bare "and cash?" occasionally routes softly. If a step doesn't land, rephrase with the explicit name + verb — e.g. "show me the cash position for Whitman." The system is built to err toward fetching or a clean clarify — it never returns a confidently wrong answer, so a re-ask is always safe.

  • Clarify didn't fire in Act 1b? You weren't in a fresh chat. Open a new conversation and re-send — clarify only triggers with no established subject.
  • An entity switch got sticky? Name the client explicitly ("Calderon Trust holdings") to force the focal entity.
  • Resilience partial looked like a full failure? Confirm only conduit-wealth-http was stopped (servicing must stay up to answer), then docker start it before moving on.
  • Wrong persona's data? You didn't fully log out between characters — end the Axiom session (full logout), then log in fresh.

Reset between characters

Always log out fully when switching people — this ends the Axiom SSO session, not just the chat. A half-logout can carry the previous identity and muddy the entitlement story, which is the one thing you can't afford to fumble in this demo.
