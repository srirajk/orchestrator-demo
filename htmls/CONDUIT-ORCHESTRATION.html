Why not a chatbot

A chatbot answers. Conduit orchestrates — and stays accountable.

The difference is where the intelligence sits. The model chooses the goal; deterministic code does the planning, the entitlement, and the arithmetic-of-record.

A chatbot

  • Generates the whole answer, including the numbers.
  • May invent an identifier, a figure, or a threshold.
  • No native notion of who is allowed to see what.
  • The plan, if any, is improvised inside the prompt.

Conduit

  • The LLM picks only the goal — one classification, not a plan.
  • Deterministic code builds the execution DAG from typed input/output contracts declared in manifests.
  • Every step runs under the user's entitlements.
  • Every number is grounded in an agent's output — the model summarizes, it never computes.

Why the model doesn't draw the graph. Asking an LLM to emit a whole execution DAG is unreliable: measured exact-graph match for frontier models sits around 0.18–0.24, while classical / deterministic planners produce valid graphs roughly 98% of the time. So Conduit lets the model do the one thing it is good at — reading intent — and derives the graph deterministically from declared contracts.
